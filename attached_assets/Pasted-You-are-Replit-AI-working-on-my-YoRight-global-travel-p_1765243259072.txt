	1.	Payments, refunds, and financial flows
Implement a robust payments and settlement layer that goes far beyond a simple checkout:

	•	Support multiple payment gateways and processors, with a payment routing engine that can:
	•	Select a gateway based on country, currency, card type, risk score, and uptime.
	•	Fallback to secondary gateways if the primary one is failing.
	•	Track success and decline rates per gateway for future optimization.
	•	Design a payment state machine that covers:
	•	Initial authorization, capture, partial capture, void, refund, partial refund.
	•	Clear separation between payment status and booking status.
	•	Idempotency for all payment operations so retries do not double charge.
	•	Build structures for:
	•	Full refunds and partial refunds (for some nights only, or some rooms only).
	•	Multiple payments per booking (split payments across cards or people).
	•	Surcharges or fees that depend on payment method or country.
	•	Implement a system for vouchers, gift cards, and store credit:
	•	Unique codes with configurable balance, expiry date, and allowed usage rules.
	•	Ability to issue credit as compensation or promotional reward.
	•	Correct accounting of remaining balance and linkage to bookings where it was used.
	•	Include tax handling and invoicing capabilities:
	•	Abstract tax logic so that different jurisdictions can define their own rules (VAT, city tax, tourism tax, etc).
	•	Support multiple tax components on a single booking and store them explicitly.
	•	Generate tax invoices with unique numbers and store immutable invoice records.
	•	Prepare for future compliance with financial regulations:
	•	Architect for separation between operational database and financial ledger.
	•	Make all financial events append only and traceable, suitable for audits.

	2.	Risk, fraud, and trust layer
Design and implement a dedicated risk and trust system for bookings and payments:

	•	Build a risk scoring engine that evaluates each booking and payment attempt using:
	•	Device and browser fingerprint.
	•	IP location vs card holder billing country vs trip destination.
	•	Velocity checks across time (how many attempts per card, per user, per device, per IP).
	•	Historical behavior of the user and of similar patterns.
	•	Allow the risk engine to output actions such as:
	•	Allow instantly.
	•	Challenge with extra verification steps.
	•	Hold booking in a “manual review” queue.
	•	Reject and block further attempts from high risk actors.
	•	Implement a manual review console for the operations team:
	•	List of bookings in review with all relevant signals and history.
	•	Ability to approve, cancel, or request more information from the user.
	•	Audit trail of who took which decision and why.
	•	Integrate with payment processors and banks for dispute and chargeback handling:
	•	Store all metadata needed for disputes.
	•	Provide internal tools to annotate disputes and outcomes.
	•	Link disputes to risk rules for future tuning.
